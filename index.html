Read these alongside the numbers:

  • Oud-Oost rests on a single stationary sensor. Its entire before/after story comes from one fixed location, so treat it as a case study rather than a definitive neighbourhood average. Noord-West is backed by 2–3 stationary sensors.
  • Season-matched ≠ raw split. The headline and the map polygons use 2023-vs-2024 month-matching to remove seasonal bias. A naïve split on 8 Dec 2023 puts a winter-heavy stretch on the "after" side and can flip the sign of small changes — e.g. Noord-West PM₂.₅ reads −2.6% (improved) season-matched but +1.5% (worsened) on the raw split. The raw figure is kept in each area's popup for transparency, and the individual sensor markers still use the raw split.
  • PM₂.₅ has ~6% missing calibrated values (the RIVM model can't always produce one); these are excluded from every statistic, not zero-filled.
  • High-tail outliers are kept (e.g. fireworks, Saharan dust). Medians are computed alongside means so a few spikes don't distort the trend.
  • PM₂.₅ is dominated by regional/secondary sources, so a local speed limit is not expected to move it much — useful context for the Oud-Oost PM₂.₅ result.
